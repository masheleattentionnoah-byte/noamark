Beginner Business Guide

Everything you need to know to go from zero to your first rand earned. No experience required.

What is a business model?
▸ START LESSON
What is a business model?
A business model is simply how you make money. You have a product or service, you find customers who need it, and you charge them more than it costs you to provide it. The difference is your profit.
How to find your first customer
Start with people you know, friends, family, neighbours. Tell everyone what you are doing. Post on WhatsApp groups in your area. Your first customer is closer than you think.
How to price your product
Add up what it costs you to make or provide it (materials, transport, time). Then add your profit margin, at minimum 30%. If the market pays more, charge more.
Managing your first income
Open a separate account or use a separate wallet for business money. Never mix personal and business funds. Track every rand in and out from day one.

Money Basics

Learn how profit, expenses, and cash flow work so you never lose money running a business.

Revenue vs profit
▸ START LESSON
Revenue vs profit
Revenue is all the money coming in. Profit is what is left after you pay all your costs. A business can have high revenue and still lose money. Always focus on profit.
Fixed vs variable costs
Fixed costs stay the same every month (rent, subscriptions). Variable costs change with how much you sell (ingredients, packaging). Know both so you can plan.
Reading your numbers daily
Every day, write down what you earned and what you spent. A simple notebook works. You cannot improve what you do not measure.
When to reinvest
Reinvest when your business is consistently profitable, not before. Put money back into things that directly help you earn more: stock, tools, or marketing.

Startup Mistakes to Avoid

The most expensive lessons, learned by others so you don't have to. Real stories, real mistakes.

Overspending before earning
▸ START LESSON
Overspending before earning
Do not buy equipment, uniforms, or a fancy setup before you have paying customers. Start lean. Prove the business works first, then invest in it.
No daily tracking
Not tracking your money daily is the fastest way to fail. You will not notice small leaks until they have drained everything. Start a daily record from day one.
Selling to wrong customers
Know exactly who your customer is. Trying to sell to everyone means you sell to no one. Pick one type of customer and serve them extremely well.
